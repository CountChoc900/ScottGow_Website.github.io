About Me

I am a University of Calgary Kinesiology graduate with aspirations of working as a physician.
My passions are geared towards helping improve health and wellness within my community,
and my goals are to motivate people to discover their own love of exercise and daily movement.

My love for being active in the outdoors is cultivated by over 20 years of biathlon training,
with over a decade spent on the Biathlon National Team representing Canada at two
Winter Olympic Games, and my knowledge of physical activity, physical literacy and
exercise physiology is informed bytime spent learning and researching within the
amazing Faculty of Kinesiology.

This website serves as a living resume that highlights my skills and accomplishments,
both academic and athletic, as well as my interests and hobbies I enjoy pursuing.
I invite you to browse my site, and please feel free to contact me with any
and all questions you may have.

Honours Thesis

My Honours Thesis studied the relationship between VO2, metabolic power and Stryd running power on differing inclines, and sought to determine whether running efficiency is constant across varying terrain. This would have implications on the ability to use running power for exercise prescription.
